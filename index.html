  • Dota 2
    Thunder's COT RPG REWORK
    Developed as a custom game mode for Dota 2 by Valve, it has received over 48.000 ratings on Steam with a 4 out of 5 star rating and has over 160.000 active subscribers. It takes a modern approach on the classical RPG elements within the Dota universe and allows players to enjoy an arsenal of custom heroes, items and a massive map filled with dangers, such as dangerous bosses and an arena where they face of an endless amount of enemies.   • SourceMod
    SourceCBL
    SourceCBL was one of the first community contributed global ban lists for SourceMod games such as Team Fortress 2 and CS:GO. It allowed server owners to upload a plugin to their SourceMod directory and it would prevent known cheaters from connecting to their servers. Bans were determined on a case by case basis and solid proof was needed to issue a ban, which were handled by multiple people. Coded in SourcePawn.
  • Team Fortress 2
    Round Roulette
    Round Roulette is a custom plugin for SourceMod servers in Valve's Team Fortress 2. It randomizes an effect from a long list of effects each round, altering the way the game is played and forcing players to adapt. Coded in SourcePawn.
  • Team Fortress 2
    TF2 Murder
    Round Roulette is a custom plugin for SourceMod servers in Valve's Team Fortress 2. It's a game where there's a sheriff and a murderer, and the sheriff must figure out who the murderer is before everyone is killed. Coded in SourcePawn.
  • Discord
    BotDocs
    BotDocs is a documentation tool for Discord bots, which allows developers to list their bot commands. Comes with a search function, filtering, sorting and much more. This was created before Discord implemented their own feature directly into the Discord client. Coded in JavaScript using React.
  • Dota 2
    Ultimate Boss Arena
    Developed as a custom game mode for Dota 2 by Valve, it's developed as an alternative to the once very popular genre called Angel Arena. In Ultimate Boss Arena, players fight 5vs5 and have to farm items, kill bosses and fight each other in an arena every few minutes. The team to reach the kill goal first, wins the game. Coded in Lua and TypeScript.
  • Hero Siege
    Hero Siege Trading
    HST (also known as Hero Siege Trading) was a website where players from the game Hero Siege could come together and list their in-game items for sale, or what they wanted to purchase from other players. It had no actual connection to Steam or the game itself and relied on players to meet up and trade in-game. Coded in JavaScript using React.
  • Hero Siege
    HSBuilds
    HSBuilds was a website for the game Hero Siege. The goal of the website was to allow players to post their in-game builds, guides, list their items for sale and provide players with information on items and other in-game items. It was the only build website for the game at its time. Coded in PHP using Laravel.
